Notice by using the plain anchor link set, when you jump to the target section the top of the section is covered up. This happens whenever you have a floating element at the top of your page (ALA v17 masthead, and the v18 site menu nav).

Now, notice on the link set that have the widget on them, when jumping to the target sections the jump position is offset such that the section appears just below the floating element.

These do not have the widget on them.

  • Goto section 1
  • Goto section 2

Code snippet for above

				<ul class="ibm-link-list">
					<li><a class="ibm-anchor-down-link" href="#section1">Goto section 1</a></li>
					<li><a class="ibm-anchor-down-link" href="#section2">Goto section 2</a></li>
				</ul>

These have the widget enabled on them.

  • Goto section 1
  • Goto section 2

Code snippet for above

				<ul class="ibm-link-list" data-widget="adjustanchorlinks">
					<li><a class="ibm-anchor-down-link" href="#section1">Goto section 1</a></li>
					<li><a class="ibm-anchor-down-link" href="#section2">Goto section 2</a></li>
				</ul>

Official standards & guidelines

ibm.com/standards/web/design/anchor-links/


What

A little utility for use when you have both a site menu nav and in-page anchor links. It will jump to the anchor target section but with an offset to account for the floating nav.


Why

For page owners that are concerned that their in-page anchor links take the user to a section but the top of the section is covered by the floating site menu nav. This will alleviate that issue by offsetting the position to account for the content that the floating menu nav covers up.


How

Just add the data-widget="adjustanchorlinks" attribute to your link list, or container of links.
